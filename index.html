Frequently asked

Questions firms usually ask first.

How do you handle confidentiality and conflicts?
Our standard NDA is countersigned within the same business day and precedes any disclosure of matter detail. Conflicts checks are run against our active engagement register before a paralegal is assigned. Privileged work is isolated to the assigned paralegal and supervising solicitor only.
Who supervises the work?
All deliverables pass through a senior Malaysian solicitor before they reach your firm. Your engagement letter names the supervising solicitor for the matter. Where a brief involves specialist subject matter, an additional senior reviewer with that expertise may be assigned.
What engagement models do you offer?
Three: hourly engagement (from RM 200/hr) for ad-hoc work; per-matter fixed-fee quotes for defined scopes such as DD reports or bundles; and dedicated retainer (from RM 15,000/month) for a paralegal embedded with your team. All models begin with an engagement letter and written scope.
How quickly can you commence work?
Most briefs are scoped and a paralegal proposed within 24 hours. For firms on existing retainer, urgent briefs can commence the same day. Court-driven deadlines are flagged on intake and prioritised accordingly.
What are your paralegals' qualifications?
All hold an LLB or equivalent law degree. Many have completed the Certificate of Legal Practice (CLP) or are pursuing chambering. Several hold LLM specialisations. CVs and writing samples are made available to engaging firms under NDA.
Do you carry professional indemnity cover?
Yes — our supervising solicitors carry professional indemnity insurance to standards required of Malaysian Bar members. Cover applicable to your matter is specified in the engagement letter. Certificates of currency are available on request.
How is billing handled?
Hourly engagements are billed monthly with itemised time records. Per-matter fixed fees are invoiced 50% on engagement and 50% on delivery, unless otherwise agreed. Retainers are billed monthly in advance. All invoices issued in Ringgit Malaysia (MYR); other terms can be agreed for international firms.
